Pope’s Message to Indonesia

I would also like to express my sincere gratitude to the President for his warm invitation to visit this country and for his warm words of welcome. I would also like to convey my sincere wishes to the President-elect for success in serving Indonesia, a vast archipelago with thousands of islands connected by the ocean. Thank you. We can say that just as the sea is a natural element that connects all the islands of Indonesia, so too is the mutual respect for the cultural, ethnic, linguistic and religious distinctiveness of all the human groups that make up Indonesia, which is the fabric that unites and makes the Indonesian people proud.

Your country is very unique, with the motto “Bhinneka Tunggal Ika” meaning “Different but still one,” which well describes this reality. The diversity that Indonesia possesses is like a beautiful mosaic where each piece has an irreplaceable role in forming an original and precious masterpiece.

This diversity enriches the nation and is a real source of beauty and strength. However, this diversity can be a threat if not respected. True harmony is created when every ethnic and religious group participates with a spirit of brotherhood, pursuing the noble goal of serving the common good. The awareness that everyone contributes to a shared history, where solidarity between all sections of society is the basis for finding appropriate solutions and transforming conflicts into positive cooperation.

Maintaining a wise balance between the diversity of cultures, ideologies and beliefs is a challenge that must be faced wisely. It is a job that requires attention and dedication from everyone, especially from political leaders who aim to create harmony, justice and sustainable development, and pursue peace both at home and with other nations. As a wise man said, politics is the highest form of love.

The Catholic Church is committed to strengthening interreligious dialogue, which is essential to eliminating prejudice and creating a climate of mutual respect and trust necessary to face common challenges, including fighting extremism. Together, we can overcome all forms of violence in the name of religion and build a more harmonious society.

Let me refer to the preamble to your 1945 constitution, which offers valuable guidance on the direction that a democratic and independent Indonesia must take. The constitution states that Indonesia is based on God Almighty and prioritizes social justice for all its people. These principles, together with unity in diversity, social justice, and divine blessings, are a solid foundation for building a prosperous nation.

However, in the midst of today’s world developments, we see several tendencies that hinder the development of universal brotherhood. Violent conflicts often arise from a lack of mutual respect and a desire to enforce personal interests at the expense of the common good. On the other hand, there are countries that try to equalize everything, including in matters that should be personal or group matters.

We must remember that the wisdom underlying the organization of the Indonesian state teaches balance and harmony. All citizens, especially leaders, must continue to be inspired by these principles in carrying out their duties, because justice and peace are the fruits of justice. Harmony is achieved when everyone works not only for personal interests, but also for the common good, by building bridges and strengthening cooperation to overcome all forms of poverty, both morally, economically, and socially.

Dear brothers and sisters, continue to walk this beautiful path, which brings blessings to all people. May God bless Indonesia with peace and prosperity for a hopeful future.
